Red Buttons Full Overview and Wiki

The Early Days and Rise to Stardom

Aaron Chwatt, better known by his stage name Red Buttons, was not just another face in the crowd.

Born to Jewish immigrants in Manhattan, his fiery red hair and a job as a bellhop donning large, shiny buttons, laid the groundwork for a nickname that would become legendary.

His early years were marked by a hustle that saw him transition from the Borscht Belt’s stages to Broadway, showcasing a knack for comedy and drama that would define his career.

Broadway and Beyond: A Diverse Portfolio

His Broadway debut in Vickie alongside José Ferrer and Uta Hagen was just the beginning.

His stint with Minsky’s Burlesque and the unfortunate timing of his Broadway show set to open right after the Pearl Harbor attack showcased his resilience.

WWII saw him entertaining troops and participating in the Army Air Forces’ Broadway show Winged Victory, further honing his craft amidst the chaos of war.

The Golden Era of Television

1952 marked a pivotal turn with The Red Buttons Show, catapulting him into television stardom.

This venture, coupled with his hit record Strange Things Are Happening, solidified his place in the entertainment industry, expanding his reach and undoubtedly his financial earnings.

Oscar Glory and Film Success

Sayonara was a game-changer for Red, earning him an Oscar and a Golden Globe, proving his versatility and depth as an actor.

His role as Joe Kelly showcased a dramatic prowess that contrasted his comedic roots, earning him critical acclaim and likely a significant financial boost.

Personal Life and Death

His personal life was marked by love, loss, and the joys of family. He married three times, with his last marriage to Alicia Prats lasting nearly four decades until her death in 2001.

This union blessed him with two children, Adam and Amy, who added a familial warmth to his bustling career. Beyond the screen and stage, Buttons was a man of community, actively participating in the Synagogue for the Performing Arts.

His life journey came to a close on July 13, 2006, when he passed away due to complications from cardiovascular disease, surrounded by loved ones at his home in Century City, Los Angeles.

Legacy and Lifetime Achievements

Throughout his career, Buttons showed an unparalleled versatility, transitioning smoothly between genres and mediums.

His performances in films like The Longest Day, They Shoot Horses, Don’t They? and Pete’s Dragon, along with television appearances up until shows like ER, showcased a longevity in the industry that few could rival.

FAQs about Red Buttons

Who was Red Buttons?

He was an American actor and comedian, known for his quick wit, comedic timing, and distinctive red-haired appearance. He made significant contributions to both film and television over his long career.

Why was he called Red Buttons?

Born Aaron Chwatt, he earned his famous nickname from a bellhop character he played in a New York City nightclub, combined with his red hair, which led him to be known as Red Buttons.

What are some of his most famous roles?

Buttons is perhaps best known for his Academy Award-winning performance in the 1957 film Sayonara. He also had memorable roles in The Poseidon Adventure (1972), Hatari! (1962), and on his own television show, The Red Buttons Show.

Did he win any awards for his acting?

Yes, he won an Oscar for Best Supporting Actor for his role in Sayonara. His performance in this film also earned him a Golden Globe Award.

Was he only an actor?

While known primarily for his acting, Buttons started his career in entertainment as a comedian. He also sang and recorded several novelty songs over his career.

What was Red Buttons cause of death?

He passed away on July 13, 2006, due to vascular disease. He was 87 years old.

Did he serve in the military?

Yes, he served in the U.S. Army during World War II.

What was The Red Buttons Show?

The Show was a television variety show that featured comedy sketches and musical performances. It aired from 1952 to 1955 and helped cement Buttons’ status as a talented comedian.

How did he contribute to the entertainment industry?

Beyond his acting roles, Buttons was known for his stand-up comedy and television performances, contributing to the development of comedic styles in both mediums. He was also a pioneer in the early days of television comedy.

What was his early life like?

He was born on February 5, 1919, in New York City. He began his entertainment career at a young age, working in burlesque and performing as a stand-up comedian before transitioning to acting.
