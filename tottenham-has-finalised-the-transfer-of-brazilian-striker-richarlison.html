Tottenham has finalised the transfer of Brazilian striker Richarlison

Posted by Tandra Barner on Sunday, August 11, 2024

Richarlison announced a 5 contract with Tottenham on Friday, relocating to London following four years at Goodison Park. According to British media, the transfer cost was 60 million pounds ($73 million). Check out complete details about it below.

Tottenham has finalised the transfer of  Brazilian striker Richarlison

The player  made 152 appearances in all games for Everton, earning 53 goals. He has 48 Premier League goals in 173 Premier League games for Everton and Watford, which he transferred from Fluminense in 2017.

Richarlison is Tottenham’s fourth acquisition of the summer, after Fraser Forster, Ivan Perisic, and Yves Bissouma. He gives boss Antonio Conte another offensive option after simply relying on Harry Kane and Son Heung-min for strikes.

Following qualifying for the Champions League with a top-four finish previous season. Upgrades have always been going to be needed at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

Ivan Perisic was the club’s first summer addition, with the Croatian star familiar with Conte’s workings from their time with each other at Inter.

With his great experience, England international goalkeeper Forster will provide healthy competition and protection for club captain Hugo Lloris.

Mali international midfielder Bissouma had been sought by a number of Premier League clubs before Spurs rushed in with a £25 million ($30 million) agreement to bring him in from Brighton.

He is seen as a significant boost in Conte’s engine room, with further reinforcements on the way.

Richarlison de Andrade debuted with América Mineiro in 2015, achieving advancement from the Campeonato Brasileiro Série B in his debut season until joining Fluminense. In his two years with the club, he played 67 games and scored 19 goals. And he was nominated to the Team of the Season as the squad became runners-up in the 2017 Campeonato Carioca.
