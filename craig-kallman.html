Craig Kallman

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 31, 2024

Craig Kallman is an American who began his professional career as a DJ. He is the current CEO of Atlantic Records as well as a businessman.

He began his career as a DJ in 1986 and founded Big Beat Records in 1987. Since 1991, he has been with Atlantic Records. He has made substantial investments in a variety of records.

Where was Craig Kallman born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Craig Kallman was born on August 13, 1965, in Manhattan, New York. Because he was constantly exposed to music, he grew up loving it. Kallman’s father had an electric record player, and he was always with him, helping him with his record collection.

He used to tag along with his father on weekend record production jobs, where he learned by doing. It also encouraged him to pursue his chosen career. His father always pushed him to do well in school, and his father’s ambition was to be a successful businessman.

He followed his father’s advice and graduated with honors in 1987. He attended Trinity High School and worked tirelessly to obtain a position at the university level. Craig majored in liberal arts at Brown University. He was about to enroll in a business course at Harvard Business School when he decided to pursue a music career instead.

How did Craig Kallman start his Professional Career?

Craig’s career started while he was still in high school. He was in his school’s music department and occasionally DJed at nightclubs. Craig Kallman went ahead to DJ during the week while in college, but only after convincing his father that he would maintain good grades. He was also in charge of all of the shows that were held.

After graduating, he was approached by various factories for DJ work and began performing in clubs such as The Tunnel. His extensive record collection, which he always prepared, kept his audience entertained. He founded Big Beat Records in 1987, releasing singles such as “Join Hands” and “The Party.” They were also international successes.

Joining hands sold over 5,000 copies, and his second single sold over 250,000. Big Beat Records was purchased by Atlantic Records in 1991. He began his career with Atlantic Records, but only in minor roles. He collaborated with Aaliyah, Brandy, and Timbaland, among others.

Craig Kallman was given a significant role in 2002, was in charge of several activities, and helped to improve the company. He was named Chairman and CEO of this company in 2005, and he was able to sign artists like Bruno Mars, Ed Sheeran, Wiz Khalifa, and many others. He was instrumental in Atlantic’s rise to prominence in the soundtrack industry.

Big Beat Records was relaunched in 2010 with artists such as Icona Pop, Chromeo, and Martin Solveig. In 2004, he released a book with the 15 best records he’d ever made.
