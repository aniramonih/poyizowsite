Early Life & Biography

Grace Latoya Hamilton, popularly known as Spice, was born on 6th August 1982 in Spanish Town, St. Catherine, Jamaica. The family later moved to Portmore. The family met with a tragedy when her dad died when she was nine years old. She later moved to London and lived with her grandparents in Finsbury Park, London. She was a part of her church’s choir as she was the leader of the choir.

She did her primary education in London and returned to Jamaica and did her high-schooling in St. Catherine High School. She also joined the Jamaica Cultural Development Commission Festival and got many medals for her participation in the festival’s music category. She realized that singing is her passion, and she wanted to make singing her career, so she decided to enter the Edna Manley College of the Visual and Performing Arts and graduated with a degree in music and drama. In her later years, she got interested in dancehall and even deejayed in some local shows.

Personal Life

Spice dated Nicholas Lall in 2006, and they got engaged in 2009 when Spice gave birth to their first son Nicholas Jr. On 22md August 2007. The couple was blessed with their second child, a girl named Nicholatoy on 21st January 2011. The had a very happy relationship, and their wedding rumors were all over the air. But, unfortunately, the couple called it quit in 2016.

Career

She started her career in 2000 by performing at the annual dancehall festival Sting. People appreciated her style and performing abilities, and hence she grew to fame. She met Baby Cham while being on tour in the UK. Baby Cham was impressed with her, and he introduced her to Dave Kelly, a producer. Dave helped her getting a deal with the Madhouse Records label, and she released her first album “Complain” under that label.

She continued to collaborate with artists like Vybz Kartel, Foxy Brown, Busta Rhymes, Mya, and many more. She even worked for records like VP, Spice Offical Ent. And Ranch. She recorded 85 singles and released 65 music videos in her entire career.

Some of her record-breaking singles are: “Right There,” “ Fight Over Man,” “Romping Shop,” “Slim vs. Fluffy,” “ Jim Screechy,” “ Body Great,” “ Hide & Do It,” “ Crosses,” “ Dear Honorable,” “ Nah Tek No Talk,” “ Cyaan Manage It,” “ Back Way” and many more.

Awards & Achievements

She has won twice the Female DJ of the year award for her work in Herself in the year 2009 and 2010 in EME awards. She even won the Dancehall Single of the Year in 2016 for her work Needle Eye and the Top Female Artist for her work herself in 2017 in 21st Hapilos Digital Music Awards.
