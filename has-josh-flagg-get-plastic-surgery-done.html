Who Is Josh Flagg?

Josh Flagg is a multifaceted American personality, encompassing roles as a real estate agent, television personality, author, and contributor to various real estate publications and news outlets.

Born to Michael and Cindy Flagg, he holds a significant lineage, being the grandson of philanthropist, electronics industry executive, and Jewish leader Herman Platt. Furthermore, he is the great-grandson of Benjamin Platt, the founder and owner of the nationwide Platt Music Corporation chain of stores.

Flagg’s financial success is notable, with an estimated net worth of $35 million. His career in residential real estate sales has seen him complete transactions totaling more than three billion dollars.

Best known for his captivating presence on Bravo’s “Million Dollar Listing Los Angeles,” Flagg is openly gay. He entered into matrimony with Bobby Boyd in 2017, and their marriage concluded in divorce in 2022.

Beyond his professional and personal life, Flagg actively engages in philanthropy and community service. He serves on the Board of Governors of Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles and the Los Angeles Jewish Home. Additionally, he is associated with charitable organizations such as Friends of the Israeli Defense Force and the Los Angeles Museum of the Holocaust.
