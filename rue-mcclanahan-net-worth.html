Early Life

Rue McClanahan was born as Eddi-Rue McClanahan on February 21, 1934, in Healdton, Oklahoma. She was raised Methodist in Ardmore, Oklahoma, and attended Ardmore High School. Career

McClanahan began her acting career on stage as a member of the Actors Studio. She made her professional debut in 1957 and started performing off-Broadway in New York City. Her television career took off in 1970 when she landed the role of Caroline Johnson on the soap opera “Another World.” McClanahan gained widespread recognition for her role as Vivian Cavender on the sitcom “Maude,” which ran for six seasons. However, she reached the peak of her fame as Blanche Devereaux on “The Golden Girls,” earning her a Primetime Emmy Award in 1987.
