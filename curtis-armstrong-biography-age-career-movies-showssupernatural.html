Curtis Armstrong Biography

Curtis Armstrong is an American actor best known for playing the roles of Booger in the Revenge of the Nerds movies, Herbert Viola on the TV series Moonlighting, Miles Dalby in the film Risky Business.

Curtis Armstrong Age

Curtis was born on November 27, 1953, Detroit, MI. He is 65 years as of 2018.

Curtis Armstrong Early Life

Armstrong was born in Michigan, Norma E’s son, Detroit. (Born D’Amico), a teacher of descent from Italy (Abruzzo) and Robert Leroy Armstrong. He graduated from Berkley High School in Berkley, Michigan, and then graduated from Rochester, Michigan, Oakland University. His daughter was born in 1996, Lily Armstrong.

Armstrong had a Catholic upbringing, but when he married Elaine Aronson, he converted to Judaism.

Curtis Armstrong Career

His first role was in the Risky Business film of 1983. He is best known, however, for his role as Booger in Nerds Revenge and his sequel Nerds II Revenge: Nerds in Paradise. In the 1992 television film Revenge of the Nerds III: The Next Generation and the 1994 television film Revenge of the Nerds IV: Nerds in Love, he later reproduced his role as Booger. In The Simpsons episode “E-I-E-I-(Annoyed Grunt),” Armstrong’s typecasting in the role was mocked. His other movies include Better Off Dead, Big Bully, One Crazy Summer, Bad Medicine, Van Wilder’s National Lampoon, Smokin’ Aces, DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story, Jingle All The Way, Southland Tales, and My Horses Beer.

In the television series Moonlighting, he also played a recurring role as Herbert Viola and played Ahmet Ertegün’s role in the biographical film Ray (2004).
On Disney Channel’s original series The Emperor’s New School And The Buzz on Maggie, Armstrong gave voices to Mr. Moleguaco and Mr. Bugspit. Also in Akeelah and the Bee was Curtis. In the 1997 cult-comedy film Elvis Meets Nixon, he played “Farley,” a fictional composite character based on members of the real entourage of Elvis Presley. He also played a bit as “Russ” on Reaper’s short-lived television show.

He’s voicing “Snot” on American Dad’s animated sitcom!To parody his role from the Nerds ‘ Revenge. He also played a role in the Stroker and Hoop cartoon series on Adult Swim as Double Wide. He appeared as Mr. Welch in Akeelah and the Bee’s 2006 production. He was playing his first role on VH1’s 100 Greatest Teen Stars despite being thirty. In the 2007 movie, Smokin’ Aces, he played “Mecklen.”

He had the top part of his head shaved to simulate male pattern baldness in the feature film Ray to prepare for his role as music executive Ahmet Ertegün. He starred in episode 10 — “Much Too Much” — and in season two of Grey’s Anatomy had a much smaller part in episode 11— “Owner of a Lonely Heart.” On That’s 70s Show, episode 315, “Radio Daze,” Armstrong played a deejay called Jerry Thunder. Armstrong was in an episode of Boston Legal in 2006. He starred as a convenience store clerk on the iCarly episode “iStakeout” in 2008, suspected of making unauthorized copies of films. He appeared in Ratko in 2009: The Son of the Dictator, and Locker 13. He played fictional astronaut Chaz Dalton on an episode of the TV series My Name is Earl. From 2008–2013 he portrayed Dr Parker/Dr. Dawson on The Game.

In 2009, Armstrong found six premiers in a mental institution playing a paranoid character in the House season, “Broken.” Also in 2009 Armstrong appeared in American Pie Presents: The Book of Love as a teacher obsessed with the school principal, and in the movie “Gold Retrievers” he also appeared as a bumbling bandit.

In 2010, he made a guest appearance on Glory Daze’s television show and began voicing the main character on Dan V’s animated series. He appeared on the Blue Mountain State (S01E11) of Spike TV as well. He appeared on an episode of Curb Your Enthusiasm in 2011, and on season seven of The Closer has a recurring role as attorney Peter Goldman. He takes up this role in Major Crimes season four.

He appeared as himself on the TV show Rules of Engagement on October 27, 2011.

Besides his acting career, Armstrong’s affinity for Harry Nilsson’s music has prompted him to become a Nilsson’s work enthusiast. He has written liner notes for Nilsson album CD reissues and has been instrumental in preparing for these reissues for archival and a bonus track. In addition, Armstrong is a keen fan of stories from Washington Irving, Laurel and Hardy, and the Sherlock Holmes stories from Sir Arthur Conan Doyle.

Armstrong and former nerds co-star Revenge Robert Carradine is hosting the King of the Nerds series of TBS reality TV competition. The series was premiered in January 2013, pitting contestants with expertise in a variety of geek interests to see who is going to be crowned with the eponymous title.

Armstrong appeared in a New Girl episode as Dr. Foster in May 2013.

In the 9th, 10th and 11th seasons of Supernatural, he continues to play the angel Metatron in several episodes.
