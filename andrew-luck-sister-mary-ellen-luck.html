Andrew Luck’s Professional Career

Between 2008 and 2011, Andrew Luck was a member of the Stanford Cardinal football team. In 2009, he was named the team’s quarterback. Luck was named the ‘Orange Bowl Most Valuable Player’ in 2010 after leading Stanford to a victory over Virginia Tech. In addition, he was voted the ‘Pac-10 Offensive Player of the Year.’ He was the first overall choice in the 2011 NFL Draft.

He played six NFL seasons with the Indianapolis Colts, totaling 23,671 yards and 171 touchdowns. In 2019, he announced his retirement from the game.

NFL Career

Andrew Luck enters the 2012 NFL Draft as one of the most highly regarded prospects. Experts dubbed him the best quarterback since Peyton Manning, and the Colts eventually selected him. He got off to a good start, throwing a 63-yard touchdown pass in his first game.

That season, he started every game and led the Colts to 11 victories. The Colts set a new record with this performance. Luck’s good fortune extended into 2014, but he was sidelined with a shoulder injury at the start of the 2015 season. This was the start of a long series of injuries for Andrew.

He was then sidelined for the rest of the season after suffering a lacerated kidney and a partially torn abdominal muscle. During the 2016 season, he suffered a concussion before undergoing shoulder surgery. He was obliged to go to Europe for extra treatment, which caused him to miss the whole 2017 season. Luck’s final season was in 2018, and he finished on a positive note.

Andrew elected to retire after being named to the Pro Bowl for the fourth time and won the National Football League Comeback Player of the Year Award.
