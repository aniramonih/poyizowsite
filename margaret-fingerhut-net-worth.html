Early Life and Education

Margaret Fingerhut, born on March 30, 1955, is celebrated for her profound impact on the classical music scene. Educated at the prestigious Royal College of Music, Margaret studied under notable figures such as Cyril Smith and Angus Morrison.

She further honed her craft with Vlado Perlemuter in Paris and notable American instructors Leon Fleisher and Adele Marcus.

Career Beginnings and Breakthrough

Margaret’s career took off when she was named a Young Musician of the Year by the Greater London Arts Association in 1981, making her London debut at the Wigmore Hall the same year.

Her first performance at the Royal Festival Hall in 1983 marked the beginning of her ascent in the classical music world.

She quickly became known for her collaborations with top orchestras such as the London Symphony Orchestra and the Philharmonia Orchestra, under the baton of renowned conductors like Leonard Slatkin and Sir Charles Groves.
