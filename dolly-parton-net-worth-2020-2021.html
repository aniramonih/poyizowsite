Early Life

Born Dolly Rebecca Parton, she is the fourth daughter out of the 12 children born to Robert Lee Parton Sr. and Avie Lee Caroline. She was born in 1946 in the outskirts of Tennessee in an area called Sevier county. The father was a construction worker and the mother a homemaker meaning they lived in abject poverty even herself she described the level of poverty through her early songs such as “Coat of Many Colors”.

Her interest in music manifested itself when she was just six years as she used to sing in church and soon after performing in local televisions and radio. When she hit 10, she was on major radio stations in Tennessee. After graduation, she moved out to Nashville to write songs with an uncle Bill Owens where she wrote top charting singles such as “Fuel to the Flame”, and “Putt it Off Until Tomorrow”. Her very first country songs that she recorded were “Something Fishy” and Dumb Blonde”.

Career

Parton career into country music kicked off when she was signed in by RCA Victor label with the help of Wagoner whom they released “The Last Thing on My Mind” and other top 10 single duets hits for a period of six years.

Her first solo single was released in 1968 “Just because I’M a Woman” but they didn’t do so well as her duets on charts but later on “Joshua”, “Coat of Many Colors”Travelin man”, “Touch Your Woman’ became top 20 singles and performed well commercially. Other songs that Dolly released up to 1975 that did well on top 10 charts are “Jolene”, “ I Will Always Love You”, “Please Don’t Stop Loving Me” and The Bargain Store”.

Between 1975 to present Dolly has released 29 music albums. Some of these albums are Dolly (1975), Here You Come Again (1977), Dolly, Dolly, Dolly (1980), Real Love (1985), Rainbow (1987), Something Special (1995), Treasures (1996), Little Sparrow (2001), Those Were the Days (2005), Better Day (2011), Blue Smoke (2014), Pure & Simple (2016) among many others.

As an actor she has appeared in a number of films and television shows such as Straight Talk 1992, Miss Congeniality 2: Armed and Fabulous 2005, Gnomeo & Juliet 2011, Joyful Noise 2012, Dolly Parton’s Christmas of Many Colors: Circle of Love 2016, Hannah Montana among many others.
