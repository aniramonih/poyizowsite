Brier Creek Walmart in Raleigh on lockdown after 'shooting as people shelter in place while police h

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 17, 2024

A LOCKDOWN is underway at the Brier Creek Walmart in Raleigh, North Carolina after a shooting allegedly occurred, leaving shoppers to shelter in place while police hunt for a suspect.

Durham City councilmember Mark-Anthony Middleton posted to Facebook at around 5.30pm alerting others of the unfolding situation.

“Shots fired here at the Brier Creek Walmart. We are not being allowed to leave. Store is locked down.

"Shooter still believed to be outside of store. Please avoid the area.”

Raleigh Police released a statement on Twitter following reports of the shooting.

"At approximately 5:25 pm, RPD officers responded to a report of a shooting in the parking lot in the 10000 block of Glenwood Avenue. A follow-up investigation is currently underway."

Police told WRAL that one person has been taken to a nearby hospital with non-life-threatening injuries.

A suspect is not yet in custody.

The area near the Walmart store is blocked off.

In an update posted about 20 minutes after the incident was reported, Middleton wrote, "Update: Raleigh PD now sweeping store with long barreled weapons. We are all being directed out and store is officially closed for the rest of the day.
