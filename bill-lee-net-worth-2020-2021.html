Early Life & Biography

Bill Lee was born on 19 January 2019 in Franklin, Tennessee, U.S.A. He was raised on a very large cattle farm which is 1000-acres, the firm was started by his grandparents and reared Hereford Cattle.

He attended and graduated from Franklin High School, after which he joined Auburn University in Auburn, Alabama, and graduated in 1981 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering.

He became the CEO and president of his family business, which deals with construction in 1992, a position he held till 2016.

Personal Life

Lee was married to Carol Ann, who died in 2000 in a horse-riding accident. After her death, Lee was forced to take some time off his construction company in order to raise his four kids. Lee got married again in 2008 to Maria, whom he married in 2008; the couple lives in Fernvale.

Lee is a Christian and attends services at Grand Chapel in Leiper Fork.

Lee served on various positions before he got elected into office, he was among the board of trustees of Belmont University, he was the YMCA chairman of Middle Tennessee, he was the president of the Associated Builders and Contractors and also the board member of Hope women clinic.

Career

Bill Lee was the CEO and President of his construction company and family home services before his political aspirations and career. He served in the family business from 1992 until 2016. He also served as a chairman briefly.

Lee’s political aspirations began in April 2017 when he announced his candidacy for governor of Tennessee in the 2018 elections. Being a business-oriented person, he also targeted the Pro-business Republicans; he ran against Congresswoman Diane Black, Randy Boyd, and state House Speaker Beth Harwell in the Republican primaries.

He won with 291,414 securing 36.8% against his competitors. He defeated Nashville mayor Karl Dean who was the Democratic nominee in the 6 November general elections and became the 50th mayor of Tennessee. He got 1,336,106 votes, and his competitor got 864,863 votes.

Lee was sworn into office on 19 January 2019, on his first two months in office Lee did a recognizable job because he addressed the economic distress that was faced in the rural counties of Tennessee, he addressed non-discrimination in employment and also he imposed a 90 days “freeze “ in order to curb issuance of new regulations by the executive. Lee still keeps up the good work as the governor of Tennessee.
