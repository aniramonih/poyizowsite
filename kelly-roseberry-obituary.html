Kelly Roseberry Obituary: Remembering Travis Mills Foundations Vice President

Posted by Patria Henriques on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

The world recently lost a remarkable soul, “Kelly Roseberry” who was known for her dedication and commitment to making a difference. You will find all the information about Kelly and her demise in this post.

Where Will Kelly Roseberry’s Funeral Services Be Held?

Kelly of Readfield, ME and her unborn son, Noah Ryan Roseberry passed away on Saturday, October 28, 2023. Kelly Roseberry held the position of Vice President of Programming at the esteemed Travis Mills Foundation.

The Foundation is mourning the tragic and sudden death of Kelly. The organization announced in a social media post, “It’s with the deepest sadness that we share this news about Kelly Roseberry, Vice President of Programming at the Travis Mills Foundation.”

On Monday, November 6th, from 1 to 5 PM, Kelly will have visiting hours at Knowlton and Hewins Funeral Home, located at 1 Church Street, Augusta.

The funeral service for her and Noah will be held on Tuesday, November 7th at 11 AM in the Augusta Civic Center, located at 76 Community Drive, Augusta. At 1 PM, burial will take place right away in the Maine Veterans Memorial Cemetery, located at 163 Mount Vernon Road in Augusta. Friends and family are cordially invited.

A Look at Kelly McGaughey’s Journey of Love and Service

Kelly’s kind and contagious personality, together with her generous manner of showing love and giving back to those around her had a profound impact on many lives. Her warmth and kindness, which endure in her memory, will always have an impact on her family, friends and large community.

James and Kathryn McGaughey welcomed Kelly into the world on March 18, 1985, in Fort Lewis, Washington. Kelly’s admiration and gratitude for service and sacrifice were shaped by her military family’s nine moves across the United States and Germany over a fourteen-year period.

She earned a diploma in Human Nutrition, Foods and Exercise from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University in 2007 after graduating from Woodbridge Senior High School in 2003. In 2010, Kelly completed her doctorate in physical therapy at Shenandoah University.

Kelly worked for two months at the Quantico Officers Candidate School, three and a half months at the Landstuhl Hospital in Germany and the same amount of time at the Walter Reed National Military Medical Center in Bethesda, Maryland, during her physical therapy internships.

Following graduation, Kelly started having an enormous influence. She worked for seven years as a physical therapist and the coordinator of clinical education at the Military Advanced Training Center at Walter Reed Army Medical Center (WRAMC) and then later at the Walter Reed National Medical Center.

Kelly got married to Chris Roseberry in 2017 after they first met in 2015 at Walter Reed National Medical Center. In 2017, Kelly began working at the Travis Mills Foundation as the director of programs.

Her involvement in several veteran-focused NGOs adapted sports and personal and professional experiences all contributed to the growth of the Travis Mills Foundation. After 21 years of service, her husband Chris retired as a First Sergeant from the US Army.

Kelly also taught adjunct courses in physical therapy and occupational therapy at Shenandoah University, the University of New England and the University of Southern Maine. She worked as a volunteer at the Veterans Administration Hospital as a PT/Amputee Specialist.

In addition, Kelly is delighted to serve as the director of an annual Ski Camp for Amputee Kids and an instructor for adaptive skiing. She enjoyed watching Virginia Tech football, going on family trips through Maine, and skiing.
