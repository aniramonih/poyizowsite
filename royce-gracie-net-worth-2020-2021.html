Early Life & Biography

Gracie was born on December 12, 1966, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in a family of martial artists. He was born in a family of nine, seven of them being boys. His father, Helio Gracie, together with his brother Carlos founded Gracie jiu-jitsu martial arts. At an early age, he began training with his father during weekends and evenings after school. Royce began competing at the tender age of 8, and at the age of 16, he had received a blackbelt. After his decision to take martial arts as a career, his dad became his teacher.

The age of 18, Royce and his brother Rorion moved to the United States of America, where they began teaching martial arts. Today, the school that they opened in California is one of the best martial arts schools in the country.

Career

Art Jimmerson was his UFC opponent, whom he easily defeated using a style commonly known as baiana. Later he met with Ken Shamrock at the semis whom he also defeated before defeating Karate and Gerard Gordeau in the finals taking home the title. In his next UFC tournament, he successfully defended his title by defeating opponents like Minoki Ichihara, Jason De Lucia, Remco Pardoel before retaining the title by making Patrick Smith his final opponent submit to punches from top position.

His first defeat came in his UFC third tournament when he withdrew from his second match due to dehydration and exhaustion. He went on to compete in several tournaments, and his last fight in UFC was against Ken Shamrock, where the match ended in a draw.

Awards & Achievements

With a martial arts career that saw him fight best of the best in the sport, awards and nominations were not hard to come way. He won UFC1, UFC2, and UFC4 Tournament Championships. He became the first person in UFC history to win a tournament in his first tournament championship. This wasn’t the only record that he made because he also became to win most tournaments in UFC as well as fighting the longest fight in UFC history, which lasted for 36 minutes.

Other awards to his name are Fighter of the Year in 1993, 2013 Lifetime Achievement with World MMA Awards, among others. To cap it all, and he was inducted in UFC Hall of Fame in 2003 as the first inductee while his nemesis Ken Shamrock was the second inductee.
