Aubrey Plaza family, husband, children, parents, siblings

Posted by Tandra Barner on Tuesday, August 27, 2024

The daughter of financial expert David Plaza and attorney Bernadette Plaza, Aubrey Christina Plaza was born in Wilmington, Delaware. Her mother is Irish and English, and her father is Puerto Rican.

Plaza earned her diplomas from a Catholic all-girls school in 2002 and from the Tisch School of the Arts at New York University in 2006. When she was a senior in high school, she served as president of the student government and took part in Wilmington Drama League shows.

Plaza suffered from expressive aphasia and a brief period of paralysis after having a stroke in 2004, while she was in her second year of college. Today, she has totally healed.

Since 2004, Plaza has performed improv and sketch comedy at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater in New York City. Additionally, she has performed stand-up comedy at The Improv and the Laugh Factory in New York City.

In the E.S.P.N. shows “Mayne Street,” “The Jeannie Tate Show,” and the pilot episode of “Terrible Decisions with Ben Schwartz,” Plaza portrayed Robin Gibney. On CollegeHumor’s “Troopers,” she played the Princess, a satirical sci-fi character.

Plaza’s saxophone had its premiere in Cassorla’s “Bona Fide” in 2014. However, she made her musical debut in Father John Misty’s “Hollywood Forever Cemetery Sings” in 2012.
