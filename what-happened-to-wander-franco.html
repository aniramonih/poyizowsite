What Happened To Wander Franco? What Did He Post?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

Wander Franco, a well-known character in Major League Baseball, is currently dealing with a number of issues both on and off the field. This Dominican professional baseball shortstop for the Tampa Bay Rays known as “El Patron” made headlines not only for his MLB debut in 2021, but also for a probe into his social media posts. The probe isn’t the only thing hanging over Franco. Fans and critics alike have questioned his recent benching owing to “behavior and attitude” issues and an ailment.

Wander Samuel Franco Aybar Sr., born in Ban, Dominican Republic, quickly rose to prominence in the baseball world. Baseball runs in his blood, thanks to his father, Wander Franco, who was a minor league player in the 1990s. He isn’t the only one in his family who enjoys baseball; his brothers Wander Javier Franco and Wander Alexander Franco have both played in the past decade.

What Happened to Wander Franco?

The Tampa Bay Rays issued a statement lately addressing their shortstop Wander Franco and the allegations leveled against him on social media. The team indicated that they were made aware of the social media remarks and that Major League Baseball is investigating the incident. The Rays have stated that they are taking the problem seriously and that they are in frequent contact with MLB as they conduct their due diligence.

Kevin Cash, the Rays’ manager, addressed the speculation following the team’s loss to the Guardians, saying that he was aware of it but declining to make any further comments. Cash also confirmed that Franco’s absence from the roster on Sunday was due to a prearranged day off, not any additional problems.

Instead of Franco, the Rays used infielder Osleivis Basabe, who was brought up prior to the game, which forced lefty Josh Fleming to be demoted. Franco, 22, signed a long-term contract agreement with the Rays in November 2021 and has had a strong season, receiving his first All-Star Game selection and batting.281/.344/.475 in 112 games.

What Did Wander Franco Post?

According to the Tampa Bay Times, deleted posts on X claimed Franco was in an inappropriate connection with a juvenile. It is crucial to emphasize, however, that these charges have not been proven or substantiated. On Sunday, Osleivis Basabe made his major league debut at shortstop. In the contest, the 22-year-old went 1 for 3 with a double. Basabe learned about his promotion to the major leagues during a Saturday night game with Triple-A Durham.
