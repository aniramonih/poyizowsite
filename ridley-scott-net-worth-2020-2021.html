Early Life

Ridley Scott was born on 30 November 1937, in South Shields, North East England, shortly before World War 2 broke out, to parents, Elizabeth Williams and Colonel Francis Percy Scott. Since Ridley’s father was an officer in the Royal Engineers; he used to be away from his family for long durations, while Ridley was growing up. Ridley Scott was the second child born to his parents with Frank Scott being his elder brother who joined the British Merchant Navy when Ridley was still very young due to which he grew up mostly without the presence of an earlier male figure in his early life.

However, he did have his younger brother, Tony Scott for a company which also went on to become a film director. Unfortunately, he lost Tony to suicide in 2010. During World War 2, Ridley’s family moved around a lot before shifting back to their native North East where they settled in Green Beck’s Road on Hartburn, County Durham.

Ridley Scott received his school education from the Grange Field Grammar School before eventually graduating from West Hartlepool College of Arts with a diploma in design. Later on, Riley went on to study further at the Royal College of Arts in London, which is where he developed his passion for films and contributed immensely to the college’s film department, making it better with time. He produced his first short film, “Boy and Bicycle” starring his younger brother, Tony and his father.

Ridley finally graduated from there in 1963 and procured the job of a trainee set designer with BBC where he worked for two years on the most famous police series of that time “Z-cars.” He ultimately got replaced by Raymond Cusick, after which he went on to establish his film and commercial production company, along with his younger brother, Tony in 1968.

Ridley Scott has been married and divorced twice. He is the proud father of three children named Jake, Luke and Jordan Scott, all of whom are somehow involved in the film business.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

Ridley Scott’s actual career began with the establishment of his own production company in 1968. His first successful movie, which received several nominations at the Canne’s Film Festival, was the 1977 starrer, “The Duellist’s.” The movie later went on to bag the Best Film award. Later, Ridley went on to create movies, like “Alien and Alien: Convenient” and “Blade Runner,” which was a flop at the time of release, but later attained a cult status. After this, he directed a blockbuster movie with Tom Cruise in the starring role titled “Top Gun” in 1986, which again brought him the Best Director nomination.

Another of Ridley’s classics was “Gladiator” which was released in 2000 and went on to clinch 5 Academy Awards, including the Best Picture and Best Actor award. After the massive success of “Gladiator,” Ridley produced many other hit movies such as “Black Hawk Down,” “American Gangster” with Leonardo DiCaprio and “Prometheus.” In 2016, he directed, ‘The Martian,’ a big-budgeted movie that became a significant hit globally and was nominated for the Oscars. Ridley’s future projects include, an upcoming series based on “Prometheus” and an impending tie-up with Disney.

Awards and Achievement

Ridley Scott has many prestigious awards to his credit among which is BAFTA fellowship, Hugo Awards, Saturn Award for Best Direction, DGA Lifetime Achievement Award, Primetime Emmy award, Bodi award for Best American Film and Gemini Award.
