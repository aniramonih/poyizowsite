Early Life & Biography

Nixon is the only child of Walter Elmer Nixon Jr., who was a radio journalist from Texas, and Anne Elizabeth, who was an actress originally from Chicago, who died in 2013. She credits her mother, who brings her into the theatre. Her grandparents were a great devotee to Lord Jesus.

Their names were Adolph Knoll, Etta Elizabeth Williams, Walter E. Nixon, Sr., and Grace Truman McCormack. When Nixon was just six years old, her parents divorced. Her father was often unemployed, and her mother was the household’s main breadwinner. Nixon’s mother worked on the show, To Tell the Truth, which was a game show.

Nixon made her first TV appearance on the show at nine as one of the “impostors.” She is pretending to be a junior horse riding champion there. All through her years at Hunter College Elementary School and Hunter College High School, she was an actress. She often took time away from school to perform in film and on stage.

Nixon also acted in order to pay her way at Barnard College. There she received a B.A. in English Literature. Nixon was also a student in the Semester at Sea Program in 1986.

Personal Life

Nixon was in a relationship with a school teacher, Danny Mozes, from 1988 to 2003. They gave birth to two children together. In June 2018, Nixon stated that their older child is transgender.

Nixon began dating an education activist Christine Marinoni in 2004. Nixon and Marinoni get engaged in April 2009. The two new couples married in New York City in May 2012. Nixon wears a custom-made, pale green dress by Carolina Herrera at her wedding. They have a son, Max Ellington, in 2011. In 2012, she identified herself as bisexual. Nixon and her family attend Congregation Beit Simchat Torah, which was an LGBT synagogue.

In October 2006, Nixon got breast cancer. She diagnosed it during routine mammography.  From then, she initially decided not to go public with her illness because she feared it might hurt her career. In April 2008, she announced her battle with the disease in an interview at Good Morning America. From that very instance, Nixon has become a breast cancer activist.

Career

Nixon’s first onscreen appearance was on “To Tell the Truth,” where her mother also worked. She began acting at the age of 12 as the object of a wealthy schoolmate’s crush in The Seven Wishes of a Rich Kid.

She made her debut with Kristy McNichol and Tatum O’Neal in “Little Darlings” in 1980. She made her Broadway debut as Dinah Lord in 1980, which was the revival of The Philadelphia Story. She did projects like the 1982 ABC movie My Body, My Child, the features Prince of the alternating between film, TV, and stage in 1981, and I Am the Cheese in 1983.

Nixon made theatrical history by simultaneously appearing in two hits Broadway plays directed by Mike Nichols in 1984, while a freshman at Barnard College. She has done “They were The Real Thing,” where she played the daughter of Jeremy Irons and Christine Baranski and Hurlyburly, where she played the role of a young woman who encounters sleazy Hollywood executives.

She played the role of Salieri’s maid/spy onscreen, in Amadeus in 1984. In 1985, she appeared alongside Jeff Daniels in Lanford Wilson’s Lemon Sky at Second Stage Theatre.

She has done her first major supporting role in a movie as an intelligent teenager who aids her boyfriend in a building in Marshall Brickman’s The Manhattan Project in 1986. Nixon was also a part of the cast of the NBC miniseries in 1988, starring Jack Lemmon and Kevin Spacey, and portrayed the daughter of a presidential candidate in Robert Altman’s political satire for HBO. She reprised the role for Tanner on Tanner, which was a 2004 sequel.
