Dan Lanning Coaching Career and Records: A Look at the Oregon HCs Accomplishments

Dan Lanning is an American football coach and former player. He is currently the head coach of the Oregon Ducks. He previously served as the defensive coordinator and outside linebackers coach at the University of Georgia from 2019 to 2021.

Lanning is considered to be one of the most promising young coaches in college football. He has been praised for his innovative defensive schemes and his ability to develop players. In his first season as head coach at Oregon, he led the Ducks to a 10-3 record and a win in the SDCCU Holiday Bowl.

Dan Lanning Coaching Career

Georgia Defensive Coordinator (2019-2021)

Lanning was named the Fain and Billy Slaughter Defensive Coordinator at the University of Georgia in February 2019. He joined the Bulldog staff after two years as an inside linebacker coach and recruiting coordinator at the University of Memphis.

During his tenure at UGA, Lanning coached some of the best defensive players in the country, including Azeez Ojulari, who was drafted by the New York Giants in the second round of the 2021 NFL Draft, and D’Andre Walker, who was drafted by the Tennessee Titans in the fifth round of the 2019 NFL Draft.

Lanning’s defenses at Georgia were ranked among the best in the country. In 2021, the Bulldogs ranked first in the SEC in scoring defense and total defense. They also led the nation in turnovers forced.

Oregon Ducks Head Coach (2022-Present)

Lanning was hired as the head coach of the Oregon Ducks in December 2021. He is the youngest head coach in the Pac-12 Conference.

In his first season at Oregon, Lanning led the Ducks to a 10-3 record and a win in the SDCCU Holiday Bowl. The Ducks finished the season ranked 10th in the AP Top 25 poll.

Lanning is expected to compete for the Pac-12 championship in his second season at Oregon. He has a talented roster led by quarterback Bo Nix and wide receiver Troy Franklin.

Dan Lanning’s Career Before Coaching
