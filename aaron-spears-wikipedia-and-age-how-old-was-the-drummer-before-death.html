Who is Aaron Spears?

Aaron Spears is one of those drummers who appears to have been born with sticks in their hands; a formidable education in drumming was provided, like so many modern American players, by playing in church, giving rise to the kind of insane chops and innate musicality that leaves drummers and non-drummers alike in awe.

Aaron Spears learned how to drum by experience as a young child in Washington, DC, mostly through his involvement in his neighbourhood church.

His stint with the Washington, DC-based Gideon Band showcased his extraordinary talent, which at some point attracted the attention of Valdez Brantley, the musical director of R&B sensation Usher.

This crucial relationship would turn out to be a game-changer for Spears’ career, allowing him to contribute to two songs on Usher’s Grammy-winning album “Confessions.”

Spears’ influence, nevertheless, went beyond his creative work. His phenomenally successful rendition of Usher’s famous song “Caught Up,” combined with his hugely successful DVD release, “Behind the Chops,” launched him into the public eye and won over a ton of admirers all over the world.

He soon established himself as one of the gospel genre’s most exceptional drummers. His ascent to the top of the music industry was facilitated by this exposure, which helped him land a number of high-profile performances both on stage and in the recording studio.

His stellar discography, which includes partnerships with well-known performers like Lady Gaga, Ariana Grande, Chaka Khan, Lil Wayne, Carrie Underwood, and many more, has cemented his reputation as a highly sought-after and acclaimed drummer in the music business.
