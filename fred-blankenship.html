Fred Blankenship Father

Just days before the celebration of fatherhood nationwide, Channel 2 Action News anchor Fred Blankenship recorded a touching tribute to his late father. Fred Douglas Blankenship Jr. or “Junior,” as he was called by friends and loved ones, lost his life at the age of 57.

“My dad always believed in me, even in times when I wasn’t necessarily sure that I believed in myself,” “Junior believed in his son and that still means the world to me,” Blankenship said his relationship with his father was most likely typical of a father-son relationship in the seventies. When his father told him to do something, he did it.

“All these conversations I have now with my two sons and my daughter now, I’m not sure they quite existed back in 1979 and 80 in Fred Douglas Blankenship Jr.’s house. I’m not sure they did,” he said. Blankenship was only 28 when his father died. He said he always felt “slightly robbed” that he didn’t have his father as their relationship moved from that of father-son to that of friends.

For Instance, he had an opportunity to traveled to California for special reports on Georgia’s water crisis comparing how the two states are dealing with water problems.

Likewise, he often hosts local leadership and community events and celebrations. Fred truly finds motive while working with kids and youth to help ensure they get added encouragement and support to achieve whatever it is they want to do in life.

Blankenship KGTV

He became part of the WSB-TV news team in 2007. Previously before joining the WSB news team, he spent almost six years working at KGTV in San Diego as both reporter and weekend anchor. While at San Diego, Blankenship won awards for stories on drug prevention and community outreach.

Many of the stories Fred covered focused on the growing problem of gangs. Also, he reported on the state’s largest fires. In 2004, Fred covered the Democratic National Convention in Boston.

Fred Blankenship  KAKE TV

Before he got hired at KGTV, he used to work as a weatherman in Wichita, Kansas at KAKE TV. During his days at KAKE, Fred chased tornados on a daily basis improving expertise at covering harsh weather.

Before he moved to Kansas, Blankenship served as a reporter at KRON in San Francisco where he was apart of a team that received the prestigious Edward R. Murrow award for the documentary “About Race”, a study of cultural and social behaviors of different ethnic groups.

To add to that, he has been a member of the National Association of Black Journalists and also a member of the National Press Photographers Association.
