The 14 Best Night Creams, Tested and Reviewed

Posted by Kelle Repass on Monday, August 19, 2024

While you get your beauty sleep, your skin is hard at work, ramping up collagen production and turning over skin cells for fresh ones to come to the surface. The best way to support your skin during this restorative process? Use a night cream. While a night mask is great for gentle revitalization and hydration, a night cream is deeply rich and is made with high concentrations of active ingredients and emollients to deeply nourish skin. They drench skin with moisture, which keeps the skin barrier protected and collagen production supercharged to help plump fine lines, firm skin, and improve your overall complexion.

There are hundreds of night creams out there and we set out to find the very best ones you can buy. We tested 20 of the most popular night creams currently available, scrutinized the creams’ consistency, absorption, and the overall effect it had on our skin over the course of two months. We also considered other important factors, like fragrance and how well it played with our other skincare products, and then ranked the effectiveness of each product based on our results, and these are the highest-scoring night creams.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Your Skin Type: When choosing a night cream — really when choosing any skincare product at all — it’s important to consider your skin type and specific needs. Here’s the good news: Almost everyone, even the oiliest of us, can benefit from a boost of hydration and moisture, making a night cream the perfect vehicle for targeted skincare. Still, what works for someone with perpetually dry skin won’t work as well on someone with oily skin, and so on. In terms of a night cream, you’ll want to use your skin type as a guide to help find the right consistency and texture for your needs. As a general rule of thumb, those with oilier skin should look for lighter formulas while those with drier skin should opt for a richer, more occlusive cream. Consider where you fall along that spectrum when choosing a night cream.
  • Your Skin Concerns: Overnight, skin is in maintenance mode, and you have the time for ingredients to really penetrate into the skin, explains Massachusetts-based board-certified dermatologist Dr. Ranella Hirsch. Consider your particular skin concerns and goals as you choose your cream. Some might be based on external factors like hormones and stress, whereas others (persistent acne, dryness, etc.) are more inherent. “Night creams usually contain active ingredients that have extra hydration or anti-aging benefits, including skin firming or brightening,” explains board-certified dermatologist Dr. Joshua Zeichner. “Look for a night cream that contains ingredients that address your particular skin needs.” For those dealing with hyperpigmentation and dullness, you’ll want to focus on ingredients that lighten age spots and dark spots and brighten the skin. Meanwhile, those with red, irritated skin should focus on soothing ingredients that will calm inflammation, Dr. Hirsch shares.
  • Active Ingredients: Once you’ve pinpointed your skin type and concerns, now it’s time to look at the ingredients. “My general advice is to start with one active ingredient at a time, especially in a richer base,” Dr. Hirsch says. “That way, if there’s any irritation, you’ll know the source.” For those dealing with dryness, look for ingredients like glycerin, oats, ceramides, and squalane. Similar hydrating and soothing ingredients are great for calming redness — ingredients like licorice root, green tea, and centella. According to Dr. Hirsch, ingredients including retinoids, antioxidants like vitamin C, green tea, and kojic acid, effectively address dullness and hyperpigmentation. Dr. Hirsch also suggests salicylic acid to help get breakouts under control, as well as congestion and oiliness, and if you’re focused on aging gracefully, look for night creams with retinoids, antioxidants, and peptides.

Your Questions, Answered

What is the difference between night cream and day cream?

Night cream tends to feature a thick base and, sometimes but not always, a combination of active ingredients meant to target specific skin concerns, Dr. Hirsch says. Because skin is in maintenance mode while you sleep, night creams are able to penetrate deeper into the skin.

“The skin undergoes normal circadian rhythms, in which specific activities occur in the morning and others in the evening,” Dr. Zeichner says. “Skin hydration has been shown to decline in the afternoon, continuing into the evening, so it makes sense to apply night cream to address the biology of the skin itself.” Your daily moisturizer is likely going to be less rich, can contain SPF, and typically serves as a better base under other products.

Is night cream different than other moisturizers?

Both Dr. Zeichner and Dr Hirsch say there are typically two main differences between night creams and other, traditional moisturizers: There’s no SPF in night creams, and they tend to have a thicker base. Because we don’t (or at least shouldn’t) wear makeup to bed, it’s a great time to apply something a bit thicker that might not sit as well under makeup. And, by putting actives in that thicker base, the occlusive properties claim to deliver more results.
