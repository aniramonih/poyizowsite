Early Life

Michael Jones was born on November 18, 1981, in Houston, Texas. He at first aspired to be an NBA player and rapped simultaneously in school. He changed many schools but ended up playing the YMCA League only owing to rules of transfer-in school. Subsequently, Jones dropped-off high school and worked at different places to make money. He bought and sold mobile phones, dime bags, T-Mobilesidekicks along with a partner.

Jones was close to his grandmother, whom he used to visit often. He acknowledged her for his rapping career and gave him ideas and suggested ways to grow as a rapper.

Career

Jones started off his musical journey by joining a band Souf Folk as the stage name of Sache and released an album with them. In 2001, he created his own record label, Ice Age Entertainment, and did independent rapping. He promoted his music on streets and in strip clubs. He was signed by an upcoming record label Swishahouse, along with other rappers, Paul Wall, Slim Thug, and Chamillionaire.

In 2004, Jones got famous for his single “Still Tippin” and “Back Then” that hit the platinum. He signed with Warner Brothers and Jive Records and came out with his debut album, “Who is Mike Jones?” in 2005. It got him into the limelight and huge fan following as it hit the peak on Billboard Hot 200.

In April 2007, he released a single “My 64” from his EP with Snoop Dogg and Bun B, which ranked No. 1 on the Hot 100 Singles chart. In November the same year, his EP and a movie, the American Dream, was released with an average response. In May 2008, his second single “Cuddy Buddy” performed well on the Billboard.

A year later, in 2009, his second album “Voice” debuted at 12th position on Billboard 200. Following some dispute with the Asylum Records, he didn’t work in 2010 and 2011. In January 2015, he came out with a mix-tape, Money Train, and announced its sequel, Money Train: Reloaded on which he was working.
