Rick Green Full Overview and Wiki

The Dawn of a Comedic Era: The Frantics

Rick Green‘s journey into the comedic world began with his pivotal role in founding The Frantics, a Toronto-based comedy troupe, in 1979. This ensemble quickly gained a reputation for their sharp wit and engaging performances, laying the groundwork for Green’s expansive career in comedy.

Television Triumphs: The Red Green Show and Beyond

Green’s creativity and comedic genius found a new avenue with the co-creation of The Red Green Show, a series that became synonymous with Canadian humor.

His portrayal of eccentric characters and the show’s unique blend of comedy, satire, and heartfelt moments captured the hearts of viewers, cementing its place in television history.

In addition to The Red Green Show, Green’s passion for blending comedy with historical insights led to the creation of History Bites. This show offered a humorous take on historical events, showcasing Green’s versatility and innovative approach to comedy.

Advocacy Through Humor: ADD & Loving It?!

Perhaps one of Green’s most impactful contributions is the documentary ADD & Loving It?!, which he wrote and directed. Featuring fellow comedian Patrick McKenna, the documentary sheds light on adult ADHD, combining humor with informative content to engage and educate audiences.

FAQs about Rick Green

Who is Rick Green?

He is a Canadian comedy writer, producer, director, and performer, best known as the co-creator of The Red Green Show, creator of ADD & Loving It?!, History Bites, founder of Rick Wants to Know, and co-founder of the comedy troupe The Frantics.

What is Rick Wants to Know?

Rick Wants to Know is a platform where he explores various subjects through videos, aiming to make life easier, happier, healthier, and more rewarding. The content is inspired by questions, problems, and requests from his patrons.

What is ADD & Loving It?! about?

ADD & Loving It?! is an award-winning documentary created by him that explores ADHD, featuring comedian Patrick McKenna. It has been endorsed by major ADHD advocacy groups and has been credited with saving lives by offering hope and understanding to those affected by ADHD.

What awards has he won?

He has won several awards, including the New York Festivals Silver World Medal for Best Medical Documentary for ADD & Loving It?! and has been appointed to the Order of Ontario. In 2017, he was appointed as a Member of the Order of Canada for his contributions to Canadian television and for raising awareness about ADHD.

What is The Frantics?

The Frantics is a Toronto-based comedy troupe co-founded by him. The group is known for their CBC radio series Frantic Times, which ran for five seasons and was nominated for 7 ACTRA Awards.

How did he start his career?

He began his career as a demonstrator at the Ontario Science Centre, where he developed educational programs. He transitioned into comedy in 1979, becoming a full-time comedian and later co-founding The Frantics.

What educational background does he have?

He has a Bachelor of Science degree from the University of Waterloo.

Has he been diagnosed with ADHD?

Yes, he was diagnosed with ADHD in adulthood, a revelation that came after his son was diagnosed with the condition. This diagnosis led him to create ADD & Loving It?! and advocate for those with ADHD.

What else has he created?

In addition to The Red Green Show and ADD & Loving It?!, he created History Bites, a comedy series that explores historical events as if television existed in the past. He also developed and directed the series, winning a Gemini award for directing.

What personal struggles has he faced?

His journey with ADHD, including his late diagnosis, has led him to advocate for understanding and acceptance of the condition. His work aims to highlight that ADHD does not correlate with intelligence or worth.
