Early Life & Biography

Nicholas Mears Loeb was born on August 2, 1975, in Purchase, Harrison, New York, the United States, to his parents John Langeloth Loeb Jr. and Meta Martindell Harrison. His father served as a Delegate to the United Nations and was a former United States Ambassador to Denmark. Loeb has one half-sister from his father’s first marriage. Popular Canadian businessman Edgar Bronfman Sr. is his uncle.

When he was one year old, his parents divorced and was raised by his father. Loeb went to the Collegiate School and Loomis Chaffee School in New York City. He also spent a few years in Denmark as his father was posted there. He later attended Tulane University, where he completed his graduation with a B.A. in management and finance.

Personal Life

Nick Loeb got married to actress Sofía Vergara in 2010. After living together for four years, the couple divorced in 2014. He has been able to keep his personal life away from the limelight and is currently focused on his career.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

Nick Loeb started his career after college working on the film Primary Colors under director Mike Nicholas which was made at his uncle’s studio Universal Studios. He produced and appeared in the 2000 drama-thriller movie The Smokers, which starred Dominique Swain, Thora Birch, and Busy Philipps. Along with Barbra Streisand, he produced the PBS documentary series The Living Century.

Loeb moved to Florida and worked with Lehman Brothers for a couple of years before founding Carbon Solutions America, which provided climate change advisory services to private as well as government clients. At the same time, he produced the country’s first carbon-neutral wine.

Loeb took part in the Delray Beach, Florida city commission race; however, he wasn’t able to win the race. In 2008, he served as a finance co-chairman for Rudy Giuliani’s 2008 presidential run. The following year, he abandoned a state Senate campaign due to divorce from his first wife. Loeb invested and used his own money to fund the campaign.

In 2011, Loeb wanted to contest for the United States Senate but later decided not to run due to health issues. He is the owner of Loeb’s Foods, which he founded in 2006. He is also the owner of the Crunchy Condiment Company, which sells Onion Crunch and fried onion topping. In 2019, Loeb appeared in the biographical film, The Brawler as Donnie Wepner. In the same year, he portrayed the role of Bernard Nathanson in the political-legal drama film, Roe v. Wade.

Awards & Achievements

In 2001, Nick Loeb was awarded the CINE Golden Eagle Award for his work in the documentary, The Living Century. He was also nominated for several other awards for the same. He is making continuous progress in his career and is expected to win big awards in the upcoming years.
