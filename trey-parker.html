Career

Trey Parker is an actor, animator, producer, and director from the United States. Parker and Matt Stone began making animated films while still in college. Only construction paper, glue, and an antique film camera were used to create ‘Jesus vs. Frosty.’ The film had its world premiere at a student film festival in 1992.

In 1993, the duo wrote and directed ‘Cannibal! The Musical’ is an indie black comedy musical loosely based on Alfred Packer’s true story. In 1995, they released another animated feature, ‘Jesus vs. Santa.’ Additionally, the story revolved around the exploits of four young gentlemen. The film gained widespread attention on the internet and attracted the attention of Comedy Central, which approached Parker and Stone about developing the ‘South Park’ television series.

The adult animated sitcom ‘South Park’ premiered on the Comedy Central television network in 1997. The show quickly gained popularity for its coarse language, sexual themes, and dark humor, all of which were geared toward an older demographic. The show quickly ascended to the channel’s top spot in the ratings. The duo was inspired to co-write and produce a feature film based on ‘South Park’ by the show’s popularity. Similarly, South Park: Bigger, Longer, and Uncut was released in 1999.

‘That’s My Bush!’ was a Parker and Stone-created comedy television series. From April to May 2001, the series aired on Comedy Central. The show was based on a fictionalized account of President George W. Bush’s private life. Parker also directed ‘Team America: World Police,’ a satirical action comedy film co-written with Matt Stone and Pam Brady in 2004. The film mocked both big-budget films and American politics.
