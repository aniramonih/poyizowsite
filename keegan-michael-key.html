Keegan-Michael Key is an American actor, writer, producer, singer, and comedian. He is best known as the co-creator and co-star of the TV sketch series Key & Peele (2012–2015) alongside Jordan Peele that aired on Comedy Central. Keegan-Michael has also appeared in TV series Mad TV (2004–2009), Parks and Recreation (2013-2015), Fargo (2014), and Playing House (2014–2017). As a movie actor, he appeared in movies Pitch Perfect 2 (2015), Don’t Think Twice (2016), Keanu (2016), and borrowed his voice to Ducky in Toy Story 4 (2019).

Education

He attended Shrine Catholic High School in Royal Oak, Michigan, United States. Keegan-Michael later attended the University of Detroit-Mercy in Detroit, Michigan, United States. In 1993, he earned his Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Acting.

He then enrolled in Pennsylvania State University School of Theatre in University Park, State College, Pennsylvania, United States. In 1996, he earned his Master’s of Fine Arts in Acting degree.
