Carly Fiorina Wiki Biography

Carly Fiorina, an American businesswoman and politician, was born as Cara Carleton Sneed on September 6, 1954, in Austin, Texas, USA. She made history by becoming the first woman CEO of the Top 20 ranked by the magazine Fortune, during her time as the CEO of the Hewlett-Packard Company from 1999 to 2006. Following her departure from HP, she ventured into politics and eventually became a presidential candidate for the Republican Party in the 2016 election.

Carly Fiorina’s overall net worth is estimated at $60 million, which she acquired through her careers. She has also published several books, including her autobiography, “Tough Choices: A Memoir” (2006).

Carly Fiorina Net Worth $60 Million

Carly’s family moved frequently in her early teens, leading her to change schools several times, including one in Ghana, before graduating from the Charles E. Jordan High School in North Carolina. She then graduated from Stanford University in 1976 with a Bachelor of Arts in philosophy and medieval history. She briefly attended the UCLA Law School before becoming a real estate broker at Marcus & Millichap Inc. She then worked as an English tutor in Bologna, Italy, before returning to the USA, laying a solid foundation for her net worth.

She graduated with an MBA in Marketing from the University of Maryland in 1980 and also holds a Master of Science degree in Management from the MIT Sloan School of Management (1989).

During the 1980s, Carly began her career as an AT&T management trainee, eventually becoming the head of the company’s ventures in North America. She was later appointed as one of the presidents of Lucent, one of AT&T’s spin-off companies, leading to a boost in her career and net worth. Her appointment as CEO of Hewlitt-Packard in 1999 further increased her net worth significantly. While her tenure was highly profitable for the company, internal conflicts led to her resignation in 2005, for which she received a compensation of about $21 million.

After leaving HP, Carly focused on her political career, working as an advisor to John McCain and as the U.S Senate Republican candidate in California in 2010, among other ventures.

Throughout her career, Carly has earned accolades and recognition, including being named the most powerful woman in business by Fortune magazine in 2003 and earning a spot on the Time 100 ranking in 2004. She was also named tenth on the Forbes list of The World’s 100 Most Powerful Women.

In her personal life, Carly was married to Todd Bartlem from 1977 to 1984 and has been married to Frank Fiorina since 1984; the couple has no children. Carly was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2009, underwent mastectomy, chemotherapy, and radiotherapy, and received a positive prognosis after the treatment.
