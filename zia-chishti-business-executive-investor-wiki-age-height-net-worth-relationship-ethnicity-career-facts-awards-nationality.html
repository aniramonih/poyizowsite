Zia Chishti is an American business executive and investor. Chishti is the CEO and co-founder of Afiniti, a business that optimizes and enhances consumer relationships with artificial intelligence.

How much is the Net Worth of Zia Chishti?

As the chief executive officer and co-founder of Afiniti, Zia Chishti has amassed enormous wealth from his company. His estimated net worth as of 2023 is $5 million, while his yearly income and compensation are being examined.

Early Years, Family and Education

American Zia Chishti was born in 1971 in Bar Harbor, Maine. He is currently 53 years old and an American citizen. In the meantime, he was given the name Wilson Lear and is of American and Pakistani descent. Muhammad Ziaullah Khan Chishti is his full name.

George Lear is his father, and it is unclear what name his mother is. In the meantime, his mother was Pakistani and his father was American. He moved to Lahore, Pakistan, with his mother when his father passed away in 1974. He was formally renamed Zia Chishti in response to anti-Christian feeling in Pakistan.

Education

He finished his education at the Lahore American School. Later, in 1988, he relocated to New York City and enrolled at Columbia University. It was there that he obtained his BA in computer science and economics in 1992. He earned his MBA in 1997 after graduating from Stanford University in California.

Professional Career of Zia Chishti:

Zia Chishti began working as an investment banker at Morgan Stanley after receiving his degree, focusing on mergers and acquisitions in both New York and London. In addition, he was employed in London by McKinsey & Company as a management consultant.

He was a co-founder and CEO of Sunnyvale, California-based Align Technology, a medical technology company, from 1997 to 1997. In 1998, Align Technologies received approval from the Food and Drug Administration to market and distribute Invisalign. With a $1 billion market valuation, Align Technology went public on the NASDAQ in January 2001.

In addition, he sold the shares he held in Align Technology when he left the company in 2003. After that, he started The Resource Group (TRG) as its first chairman and CEO. The first company that TRG bought was Align Technology’s Lahore, Pakistani business, which Align had sold off in 2001.

In July 2003, he listed TRG on the Karachi Stock Exchange. By 2005, the company had bought and supported American contact centers in addition to establishing operations in Karachi and Lahore.

In 2005, Chishti and other former employees of Align Technology co-founded Orthoclear, a startup company focused on medical devices. The business settled a lawsuit for patent infringement brought by Align in 2006, and Orthoclear received $20 million in exchange for its intellectual property. Eventually, he turned his attention to TRG.

Moreover, he created Afiniti in 2005 in Washington, D.C., with TRG holding around half of the business’s equity. In 2006, Chishti, the CEO and chairman of Afiniti, penned the first version of the software on his dining room table.

The product increases call center productivity by utilizing artificial intelligence. Chishti continued to lead Afiniti as CEO and chairman of the board, which also included directors José Maria Aznar and John W. Snow. By 2017, The Resource Group had a number of subsidiaries, including the offshore company TRG International, and it functioned as an equity vehicle.

Awards

Mamnoon Hussain, the president of Pakistan, gave him the Sitara-e-Imtiaz Award for Information Technology in March 2018. In April of the same year, Chishti was also awarded the MIT AI Innovator Award. People Magazine listed Chishti as one of the top 50 bachelors in the country in July 2001.

Relationship Status of Zia Chishti:

Zia Chishti wed Sarah Pobereskin in 2020 in Bermuda. Not much is known about their married lives. The pair hasn’t yet shared any children together.

Sexual Assault Accusations

Tatiana Spottiswood, a former Afinit employee, spoke about sexual harassment before the US House Committee on the Judiciary on November 16, 2021. It is claimed by her that in 2017 while on a business trip to Brazil, Chishti beat and shoved her.

Also, she also claimed that when she refused to take his hand, Chishti allegedly grabbed her buttocks and called her a “bitch.” On November 28, 2021, Chishti resigned from Afiniti and TRG in response to this accusation.
