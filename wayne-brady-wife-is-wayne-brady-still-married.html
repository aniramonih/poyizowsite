Wayne Brady Wife: Is Wayne Brady Still Married?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Saturday, August 17, 2024

Wayne Brady is a multi-talented American actor, comedian, singer, and television personality. Read this article to find out more about his wife. 

Wayne Brady was born on June 2, 1972, in Columbus, Georgia, United States of America. He grew up in Orlando, Florida. From an early age, Wayne showed an interest in performing and began his career in the entertainment industry as a theater actor.

His breakthrough came when he became a regular cast member on the improvisational comedy show “Whose Line Is It Anyway?” in the late 1990s. His quick wit, improvisational skills, and charismatic presence made him a fan favorite on the show.

As his popularity soared, his career diversified. He ventured into hosting and became the host of his own daytime talk show, “The Wayne Brady Show,” which earned him an Emmy Award in 2003. He also hosted various game shows like “Don’t Forget the Lyrics!” and “Let’s Make a Deal.”

In addition to his television work, Wayne Brady has showcased his musical talents on various occasions. He released a debut R&B album titled “A Long Time Coming” in 2008, which received critical acclaim.

His acting career includes appearances in numerous television series and films, with notable roles in “How I Met Your Mother,” “30 Rock,” and “The Bold and the Beautiful.” He has also taken on Broadway roles, impressing audiences with his performances in musicals like “Chicago” and “Kinky Boots.”

Throughout his career, Wayne Brady has been recognized with several awards and nominations, including Emmy Awards and a Grammy nomination for his work in the entertainment industry.

Wayne Brady Wife

Wayne Brady has been married twice in his life. He first married Diana Lasso on December 31, 1993, but their marriage ended in divorce on September 21, 1995. Later, on April 3, 1999, he tied the knot with dancer Mandie Taketa.

Together, they have one child, a daughter named Maile Masako Brady, born on February 3, 2003. Unfortunately, Brady and Taketa separated on April 5, 2006, and Taketa filed for divorce on July 2, 2007.
